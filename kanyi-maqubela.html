In my practice, I work with people who enter psychotherapy for many reasons--from general issues including depression, anxiety, and low self-esteem to specific problems such as divorce, family conflict, and career crises. Some people are dealing with a long-standing issue, while others are struggling with recent stressors. Because individuals' problems often arise in their interpersonal environments, my goal is to provide a warm and collaborative atmosphere in which people can openly explore their difficulties and feelings, better understand their personalities, and reach new resolutions and solutions. My expertise includes helping adults experiencing sadness, anxiety, loneliness, grief, life transitions, identity confusion, and relationship difficulties. I also specialize in helping people suffering from cancer and other serious physical illness, as well as their family members, cope with emotional experiences that can arise in such circumstances.
